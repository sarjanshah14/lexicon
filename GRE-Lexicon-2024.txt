                                           PREFACE

 GRE® Lexicon is a part of your GRE® classroom coaching program. It is designed to prepare
 you for GRE® Text Completion and Sentence Equivalence questions. The booklet contains words
 which have appeared in the past GRE® tests. So to prepare for your Verbal Reasoning section,
 you should familiarize and memorize yourself with all the words of this booklet.

 The booklet is divided into 31 Wordlists and each wordlist contains 25 words. Each word in the
 Wordlist is exhaustively treated to oﬀer a clear idea of all the aspects of a word–meaning, usage,
 synonyms, and antonyms. The meanings given are brief and to the point. They are often followed
 by examples–sample sentences or phrases showing how the word is actually used in speech and
 writing. The words marked with an asterisk (*) are high-frequency words that appear most often
 on the GRE®.

 While learning a Wordlist, you will invariably ﬁnd that you have already come across about
 10-15 of these words, but you hadn’t cared to learn them properly. Study these already-seen
 words carefully. The remaining words which you have come across for the ﬁrst time, will have
 to be learned more thoroughly.

 The GRE® Lexicon ends with Word Groups and Common Confusing GRE® Words.

Common Confusing GRE Words

 Following are pairs or groups of words that are similar enough to be confused with one another. Learn
 exactly what each word means, how to spell it, and use it exactly.

 1.   acrid                sharp, bitterly pungent - Acrid smell of burning hair
      acrimonious          stinging, caustic - Acrimonious argument

 2.   aﬀection             feeling of love
      aﬀectation           artiﬁcial show, insincere pretense - Her little aﬀectations annoyed me

 3.   allude               to make an indirect reference
      elude                to escape, evade

 4.   amiable              lovable, agreeable (applies to persons)
      amicable             peaceable, harmonious (applies to arrangements, settlements, and
                           relationships) - The settlement was arrived at amicably
      amenable             readily managed, willing to be led

 5.   appraise             to estimate, judge - Performance appraisal
      apprise              to inform - They apprised the police of the incident

 6.   alternate            every other - The doctor visits her on alternate days
      alternative          choice between two or more alternatives - The hapless man saw no alternative
                           before him but death

 7.   attenuate            reduce - Famished people with attenuated bodies
      extenuate            lessen the magnitude (of guilt) - Nothing can extenuate your conduct

 8.   baneful              ruinous, poisonous - Drink was the bane of his life
      baleful              deadly, destructive, sinister - Baleful looks

 9.   brusque              blunt, abrupt - Brusque manner
      burlesque            an imitation that ridicules

 10. beneﬁcial             useful - His advice proved beneficial to me
     beneﬁcent             kind - Helping the needy is a beneficent act.

 11. bridal                of wedding, of bride
     bridle                control, check - Try to bridle your passions

 12. broach                to initiate, open - Broach the topic
     brooch                an ornament
